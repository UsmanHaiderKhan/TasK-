Web Hosting Control Panel Technology

It was March 1997 when CyberSoph founder Gabriel Murphy realized the need to automate the work flow processes of CommuniTech.Net (now Web.com) as domain registrations rolled in and repeated requests for email address additions thereto. Four years later in 2001, the Personal Control Panel supported 30,000+ subscribers and $10 million of recurring revenues. This technology became the platform by which Web.com used migrate over $200 million of subscribers into through 2002-2003.

Pre-Gmail Email Platform to Consolidate Email Accounts

The brainchild of CommuniTech co-founder Bryan Heitman, FuseMail sought to provide a single user interface for checking and sending email from multiple email accounts. Launched as a B2C platform, the introduction of Gmail by Google into the market disrupted the business model, causing the platform to shift to a B2B business model. To this day, elements of the FuseMail platform are found within the SMB platform presently offered by J2 Communications.

Remote.io was sure the success of its business concept was inevitable - so they budgeted for quality and ultimately opted to have AMS built a custom back-end platform for job posting and placement. Just a year after completion of the MVP, the platform now supports thousands of employers and employee candidates throughout Europe.
